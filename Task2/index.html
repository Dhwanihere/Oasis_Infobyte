A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam


Abdul Kalam was an aerospace scientist, joined India’s defense after graduating from Madras Institute of Technology. He is known to be one of the prominent figures in the development of the country’s nuclear capabilities and is known to be a nation’s hero in building the country’s scientific power. Abdul Kalam has also served the country as a president for one term 2002 to 2007 and he died of a heart attack on July 27, 2015.

Childhood & Early Life

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Childhood & Early Life


A.P.J Abdul Kalam was born and brought up on the island of Dhanushukodi on the Southeastern coast of India. He developed early the fascination towards flights after watching birds and later it turned into aeronautics after he came across a newspaper article about the British fighter plane.

President A.P.J Abdul Kalam

President A.P.J Abdul Kalam


Abdul Kalam's dream of becoming a fighter pilot in the Indian Air Force didn't materialize, but he excelled in science. He joined DRDO in 1958, then moved to ISRO in 1969. He played a key role in India's space program and led the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, making him a national hero despite facing international sanctions.

Awards To A.P.J Abdul Kalam

Awards To A.P.J Abdul Kalam


Abdul Kalam was granted the Padma Bhushan in 1981, the Padma Vibhushan in 1990 and the Bharat Ratna in 1997. He is awarded all these as India's highest civilian awards for his beautiful contribution to the country. He has written many novels He also wrote several books, including the autobiography Wings of Fire in 1999.

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Death

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Death


Abdul Kalam suffered a fatal heart attack while lecturing at the Indian Institute of Management and passed away at the age of 83 on July 27, 2015. He was laid to rest with state honors in his native Tamil Nadu. In honor of his contributions, the Tamil Nadu government established the Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Award, recognizing talent in science and humanities.
